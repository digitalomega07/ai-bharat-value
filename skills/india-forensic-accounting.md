# India Forensic Accounting Review

Perform a source-backed forensic review of `$ARGUMENTS`. This is a risk-screening exercise, not an allegation of misconduct.

## Evidence

Use at least five years of annual reports when available, exchange announcements, audit reports, credit-rating rationales, shareholding patterns, related-party disclosures, and material subsidiary filings. Quote sparingly and cite document, date, and page where possible.

## Tests

1. Reconcile PAT, CFO, FCF, cash, debt, and interest income.
2. Trend receivables, inventory, advances, contract assets, payables, and cash-conversion cycle against revenue.
3. Inspect capitalized development costs, intangible additions, CWIP ageing, project advances, and impairment policy.
4. Review exceptional items, other income, fair-value gains, deferred tax, and recurring “one-offs.”
5. Map related parties, loans, guarantees, purchases, sales, leases, and balances.
6. Review promoter pledging, encumbrances, dilution, warrants, remuneration, and insider transactions.
7. Track auditor tenure, resignation, qualifications, emphasis-of-matter paragraphs, key audit matters, and fee changes.
8. Review contingent liabilities, tax disputes, regulatory actions, defaults, and rating changes.
9. Reconcile subsidiaries, associates, acquisitions, goodwill, minority interests, and standalone versus consolidated results.
10. Search for unexplained restatements, late filings, and inconsistent operating KPIs.

## Classification

Label each observation:

- `Green`: adequately explained and immaterial
- `Amber`: requires monitoring or better disclosure
- `Red`: material unresolved inconsistency or governance concern
- `Unknown`: evidence unavailable

Never label a company fraudulent based on ratios, Benford analysis, media claims, or one unexplained variance. State alternative explanations and the additional evidence needed.

## Output

Provide an executive risk summary, source table, five-year diagnostic table, issue register, management questions, and verdict: `No material issue found`, `Monitor`, `Needs primary verification`, or `High diligence risk`. A lack of detected issues is not proof that none exist.
